# Capital Ratios and CET1

**Topic:** Banking Regulation
**Tags:** cet1, capital ratio, risk-weighted assets, tier 1, capital buffer
**Created:** 2026-04-13
**Author:** Claude Sonnet 4.6

---

## Definition

Regulatory capital ratios express a bank's capital as a fraction of its Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA). Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) — comprising ordinary shares and retained earnings — is the highest-quality, most loss-absorbing tier. Tier 1 capital (CET1 plus Additional Tier 1 instruments such as contingent convertibles) is "going-concern" capital; Tier 2 (subordinated debt) is "gone-concern" capital that absorbs losses in wind-down.

## Key Formula

$$\text{CET1 Ratio} = \frac{\text{CET1 Capital}}{\text{RWA}} \qquad \text{Tier 1 Ratio} = \frac{\text{Tier 1 Capital}}{\text{RWA}} \qquad \text{Total Capital Ratio} = \frac{\text{Total Regulatory Capital}}{\text{RWA}}$$

Basel III minimums (plus Capital Conservation Buffer):

| Ratio | Pillar 1 minimum | + Conservation Buffer | Practical floor |
|---|---|---|---|
| CET1 | 4.5% | 2.5% | **7.0%** |
| Tier 1 | 6.0% | 2.5% | **8.5%** |
| Total | 8.0% | 2.5% | **10.5%** |

Breaching the conservation buffer triggers restrictions on dividends and bonus payments.

## Example

A bank has CET1 of £8 bn and RWA of £100 bn, giving a CET1 ratio of 8%. The 7% practical floor is met, leaving a buffer of £1 bn. If a stress test doubles the RWA of the mortgage book to £120 bn, the CET1 ratio falls to 6.7% — breaching the conservation buffer and automatically restricting distributions until capital is rebuilt.

## Remember

Capital ratios are the primary lever through which regulators control bank risk-taking: a higher RWA (e.g., from switching from a Standardised to a more conservative model) directly reduces the capital ratio and can force deleveraging. This is why banks invest heavily in IRB models that produce lower, but better-justified, risk weights — a 1 percentage point reduction in RWA density across a £200 bn balance sheet frees up £2 bn of capital that can be redeployed or returned to shareholders.
