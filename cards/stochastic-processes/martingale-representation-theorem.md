# Martingale Representation Theorem

**Topic:** Stochastic Processes
**Tags:** martingale, ito integral, complete market, hedging, replication
**Created:** 2026-03-07
**Author:** Claude Opus 4.6

---

## Definition

The **Martingale Representation Theorem** (MRT) states that every martingale adapted to the Brownian filtration can be written as an initial value plus an Itô integral. It is the converse of the fact that Itô integrals are martingales: not only does every Itô integral produce a martingale, but every martingale arises this way.

## Key Formula

If $M_t$ is a martingale with respect to the filtration generated by Brownian motion $X_t$, then there exists an adapted process $g(t, X_t)$ such that:

$$M_T = M_0 + \int_0^T g(t, X_t)\,dX_t$$

| Direction | Statement |
|---|---|
| Forward | Itô integral $\Rightarrow$ martingale |
| Converse (MRT) | Martingale $\Rightarrow$ representable as Itô integral |

## Example

Consider a European call option with discounted payoff $M_T = e^{-rT}\max(S_T - K, 0)$. Under the risk-neutral measure, the discounted option price $M_t = e^{-rt}V(S_t, t)$ is a martingale.

By the MRT, there exists a process $g(t, S_t)$ such that:

$$M_t = M_0 + \int_0^t g(s, S_s)\,dX_s$$

The function $g$ turns out to be $e^{-rs}\sigma S_s \frac{\partial V}{\partial S}$ — proportional to **delta**. The theorem guarantees this hedging strategy exists.

## Remember

The Martingale Representation Theorem is the mathematical foundation of **market completeness**. It guarantees that every contingent claim in the Black–Scholes world can be perfectly replicated by a dynamic trading strategy — the function $g$ in the representation is precisely the delta hedge. Without this theorem, there would be no guarantee that a risk-free replicating portfolio exists, and the entire framework of no-arbitrage pricing via replication would collapse. In incomplete markets (e.g. with jumps or stochastic volatility), the MRT fails, which is why perfect hedging is no longer possible.
