# Absolute Return

**Topic:** Financial Mathematics
**Tags:** absolute return, hedge funds, risk-free rate, performance, total return, target return
**Author:** Claude Opus 4.6

---

## Definition

Absolute return is the total gain or loss of an investment over a period, expressed as a percentage of the initial capital, without reference to any benchmark index. An absolute return strategy aims to generate positive returns in all market conditions — rising, falling, or flat — typically by combining long and short positions, leverage, and derivatives.

## Key Formula

The **absolute return** over a single period is:

$$R_{\text{abs}} = \frac{V_T - V_0}{V_0}$$

where $V_0$ is the initial portfolio value and $V_T$ is the final value.

Absolute return strategies are often evaluated against the **risk-free rate** rather than a market index. The **excess return** is:

$$R_{\text{excess}} = R_{\text{abs}} - R_f$$

The Sharpe ratio uses this excess return to measure risk-adjusted absolute performance:

$$S = \frac{R_{\text{abs}} - R_f}{\sigma_p}$$

## Example

A hedge fund starts the year with £200 million and ends with £218 million. The risk-free rate is 4%:

$$R_{\text{abs}} = \frac{218 - 200}{200} = 0.09 = 9\%$$

$$R_{\text{excess}} = 0.09 - 0.04 = 0.05 = 5\%$$

If the portfolio's annualised volatility is 8%:

$$S = \frac{0.05}{0.08} = 0.625$$

The fund delivered 9% absolute return, 5% above the risk-free rate, with a Sharpe ratio of 0.625. Whether the market rose 20% or fell 10% is irrelevant — the fund is judged on its own result.

## Remember

Absolute return is the philosophy underpinning hedge fund investing. While traditional fund managers can claim success by losing less than their benchmark, absolute return managers have nowhere to hide — they either make money or they do not. This is why hedge funds charge "2 and 20" fees: the promise is that skill-based strategies should deliver positive returns regardless of market direction, and the performance fee aligns the manager's incentive with the investor's actual profit rather than relative outperformance.
